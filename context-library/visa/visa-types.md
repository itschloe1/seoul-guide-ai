# Visa Types for Foreigners in Korea: A Complete Guide

> Last updated: 2026-04-10 | Sources: HiKorea (hikorea.go.kr), Korea Immigration Service, Ministry of Justice, r/Living_in_Korea, r/teachinginkorea, audience-pulse-2026-04-10

---

## Quick Answer

Korea has dozens of visa types, but most foreigners fall into one of about 10 categories. The most common confusion: **which visa lets you do what**, **how to switch between them**, and **what happens if you mess up a deadline**. The consequences of visa violations are real -- fines, deportation, and re-entry bans. When in doubt, call **1345** (foreigner helpline) before acting.

## Most Common Visa Types

### Work Visas

| Visa | Name | For Who | Duration | Can Switch To |
|------|------|---------|----------|--------------|
| **E-2** | Teaching | English/foreign language teachers at academies and schools | 1 year (renewable) | E-7, F-2 |
| **E-7** | Skilled Worker | Professionals with specific skills (IT, engineering, design, etc.) | 1-3 years | F-2, F-5 |
| **E-9** | Non-professional | Factory, agriculture, fishing, construction workers | 3 years (extendable to 4y10m) | E-7 (difficult) |
| **C-4** | Short-term Work | Temporary/contract work under 90 days | 90 days | Cannot extend in-country |

### Student Visas

| Visa | Name | For Who | Duration | Work Allowed? |
|------|------|---------|----------|--------------|
| **D-2** | Student | University degree programs (D-2-1 through D-2-8 subtypes) | Duration of program | Part-time, 20hrs/week during semester (with permit) |
| **D-4** | Language Trainee | Korean language school (어학당) students | 6 months-2 years | Limited, 20hrs/week (with permit, after 6 months) |

**D-2 Subtypes (common confusion):**
- D-2-1: Associate degree
- D-2-2: Bachelor's degree
- D-2-3: Master's degree
- D-2-4: Doctoral degree
- D-2-6: Exchange student
- D-2-8: Short-term study (1-2 semesters)

### Residence / Long-term Visas

| Visa | Name | For Who | Duration |
|------|------|---------|----------|
| **F-1** | Visiting/Joining Family | Dependents of visa holders | Varies |
| **F-1-D** | Digital Nomad | Remote workers for overseas employers (new 2025) | 1 year (renewable to 2) |
| **F-2** | Resident | Points-based long-term residence | 3 years (renewable) |
| **F-4** | Overseas Korean | Ethnic Koreans with foreign citizenship | 2 years |
| **F-5** | Permanent Resident | Permanent residence (green card equivalent) | Unlimited |
| **F-6** | Marriage | Married to Korean national | 2 years → F-5 eligible |

### Other Common Visas

| Visa | Name | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **H-1** | Working Holiday | Age 18-30 (some countries 18-25). 1 year, non-renewable |
| **D-10** | Job Seeker | For graduates of Korean universities or those seeking employment. 6 months-2 years |
| **B-1/B-2** | Tourist/Transit | 90 days (some countries), NO work allowed |

## Digital Nomad Visa (F-1-D) -- New in 2025

This is the newest visa type and generates a lot of questions.

**Requirements:**
- Employed by or contracted with a company **outside Korea**
- Annual income of at least $65,000 USD (or equivalent)
- Health insurance valid in Korea
- No criminal record
- Clean immigration history

**What you can do:**
- Live in Korea and work remotely for your overseas employer
- Bring spouse and children (dependent visa)
- Open a bank account with ARC
- Use Korean healthcare (with private insurance initially, NHIS enrollment varies)

**What you CANNOT do:**
- Work for a Korean company (that requires E-7)
- Freelance for Korean clients
- Start a business in Korea

**Duration:** 1 year, renewable once (max 2 years total)

## Critical Deadlines & Rules

### Address Reporting (주소지 신고/변경)

**When you move, you must report your new address within 14 days.**

This is one of the most commonly missed deadlines.

**How to report:**
1. Visit your local **출입국관리사무소 (immigration office)** or
2. Report online via **HiKorea** (hikorea.go.kr) -- but this often doesn't work smoothly
3. Some local **주민센터 (community centers)** can process this too

**If you're late:**
- Fine of up to 200,000 KRW
- Repeated violations can affect visa renewal
- In practice, fines are not always enforced for short delays, but don't count on this

**What you need:** ARC, new address (Korean), lease contract or proof of accommodation

### Visa Renewal / Extension

**Apply at least 30 days before your visa expires.** Do not wait until the last week.

**Where:** Immigration office (book via HiKorea) or for some visa types, online via HiKorea

**General documents needed:**
- Passport and ARC
- Application form (download from HiKorea)
- Passport photo (3.5 x 4.5 cm)
- Fee: 60,000 KRW (varies by visa type)
- Additional documents specific to your visa type (employment contract, enrollment certificate, etc.)

### Overstay Consequences

| Duration of Overstay | Consequence |
|---------------------|-------------|
| 1 day - 30 days | Fine (~200,000-500,000 KRW), voluntary departure |
| 30 days - 1 year | Fine + possible deportation + 1-year re-entry ban |
| 1+ years | Fine + deportation + 3-year re-entry ban |
| Working illegally | Fine + deportation + 5-year re-entry ban |

**If you realize you've overstayed:** Go to immigration IMMEDIATELY and explain. Voluntary reporting results in significantly lighter penalties than being caught.

## Switching Between Visa Types

### Common Visa Transitions

| From | To | Possible? | Notes |
|------|-----|-----------|-------|
| D-2 (Student) → | E-7 (Skilled Worker) | Yes | Need job offer, employer sponsorship |
| D-2 (Student) → | D-10 (Job Seeker) | Yes | After graduation, apply within 6 months |
| D-10 (Job Seeker) → | E-7 (Skilled Worker) | Yes | When you find employment |
| E-2 (Teacher) → | E-7 (Skilled Worker) | Yes | Need qualifying job + employer sponsorship |
| E-2 (Teacher) → | F-2 (Resident) | Yes | Points-based system (income, Korean ability, etc.) |
| H-1 (Working Holiday) → | E-2 or E-7 | Difficult | Usually must leave Korea and re-enter on new visa |
| Tourist (B-1) → | Any work visa | No | Must leave Korea and apply from home country |

**Important:** Most visa changes require you to **apply before your current visa expires**. Do not let your visa expire expecting to change status -- this makes everything harder.

### How to Apply for Visa Change

1. Book appointment at immigration via HiKorea
2. Bring: passport, ARC, current visa documents, new visa documents (job offer, etc.)
3. Fee: 100,000 KRW (visa status change)
4. Processing: 2-4 weeks (can be longer)

## The "Every Immigration Office Gives Different Answers" Problem

This is a real and widely reported issue. Different immigration officers may interpret rules differently.

**How to deal with it:**
- **Get it in writing.** Ask the officer to note what they told you or give you a reference number
- **Call 1345 first.** The helpline can give you the official interpretation before you visit
- **Bring all documents** even if you think some aren't needed -- better to have too many than too few
- **Go early in the day.** Officers are less rushed and more willing to explain
- **If denied, you can ask to speak to a supervisor** -- politely: "담당자님과 상담할 수 있을까요?"

## Common Q&A

**Q: Can I work while on a student visa (D-2)?**
A: Yes, but only part-time (20 hrs/week during semester, 40 hrs/week during breaks) and you need a **Part-time Work Permit** from immigration. Working without this permit is illegal and can result in deportation.

**Q: My employer hasn't sponsored my visa yet. Can I start working?**
A: No. You must have the correct visa BEFORE starting work. Working on a tourist visa or without proper status is illegal for both you and the employer.

**Q: I got married to a Korean. What visa do I get?**
A: F-6 (Marriage Migration). Apply at immigration with marriage certificate, spouse's documents, and proof of relationship. After 2 years (and meeting requirements), you can apply for F-5 (permanent residence).

**Q: Can I leave and re-enter Korea while my visa application is processing?**
A: Check with immigration. Some pending applications allow travel with a **Re-entry Permit**, others don't. Leaving Korea during processing can void your application.
