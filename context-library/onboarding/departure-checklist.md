# Leaving Korea Checklist: What to Do Before You Go

> Last updated: 2026-04-10 | Sources: r/Living_in_Korea (91+ comments departure thread), National Pension Service (nps.or.kr), NHIS (nhis.or.kr), Korean Immigration (hikorea.go.kr), National Tax Service (nts.go.kr), audience-pulse-2026-04-10

---

## Quick Answer

Leaving Korea requires unwinding everything you set up: **pension refund, health insurance, tax settlement, bank account, phone contract, housing deposit, and alien registration**. Start at least **60 days before departure**. The biggest money items are your **National Pension lump-sum refund** (you get it back) and your **housing deposit** (보증금). Both can take weeks to process, so don't leave these to the last minute.

---

## The Timeline

| When | What | Why It Matters |
|------|------|---------------|
| 60 days before | Notify landlord of move-out date | Required by most contracts for deposit return |
| 45 days before | Start pension refund paperwork | Processing takes 2-4 weeks after departure |
| 30 days before | Notify employer, check severance | Severance pay (퇴직금) if employed 1+ year |
| 30 days before | Cancel or transfer phone contract | Early termination fees apply |
| 14 days before | File final tax return if needed | Employers usually handle this, but verify |
| 7 days before | Close or convert bank account | Some banks allow overseas access, most don't |
| 1-3 days before | Report departure at immigration | Deregister your ARC at the airport |
| Departure day | Final steps at airport immigration | ARC surrender, departure confirmation |
| After departure | Claim pension refund from abroad | Can be done online or via Korean embassy |

## Step-by-Step Guide

### 1. National Pension Refund (국민연금 반환일시금)

This is often the biggest amount you'll recover. If you've been paying into the National Pension Service (NPS), you're entitled to a **lump-sum refund** when you leave Korea permanently.

**Who qualifies:** Any foreigner who paid into NPS and is leaving Korea (unless your country has a social security agreement with Korea that transfers pension credits -- check the reciprocity list).

**Countries with pension transfer agreements (NO lump-sum refund):**
USA, Canada, Germany, France, Australia, Japan, UK, and others. If your country is on this list, your Korean pension contributions transfer to your home country's pension system instead. Check: nps.or.kr/jsppage/english/agreement.

**How much you get back:**
Your total contributions + employer's matching contributions + interest. Typically this is significant -- if you earned 2.5M KRW/month for 2 years, expect roughly 5-6M KRW back.

**How to claim:**

Option A -- Apply before you leave:
1. Visit your local NPS office with passport, ARC, bank account details
2. Fill out the Lump-sum Refund Application (반환일시금 청구서)
3. Refund is processed after your departure is confirmed
4. Deposited to your Korean bank account (keep it open!) or overseas account

Option B -- Apply after you leave:
1. Submit application online via nps.or.kr or through a Korean embassy
2. Need: copy of passport, proof of departure, overseas bank details
3. Processing: 2-4 weeks after application

**Important:** You must actually leave Korea. NPS confirms your departure through immigration records before processing the refund.

### 2. Health Insurance (NHIS) Settlement

**If employed:** Your employer handles NHIS cancellation when you officially resign. Your coverage ends on your last day of employment. Verify with HR that this is done.

**If self-enrolled:** Visit your local NHIS branch or call 1577-1000 to cancel. Bring your ARC. You may have a small final bill or refund depending on your payment cycle.

**Overpayment refund:** If you've prepaid, NHIS will refund the difference to your Korean bank account. Keep your account open until this clears.

### 3. Tax Settlement (세금 정산)

**Employed workers:** Your employer performs year-end tax settlement (연말정산) as part of your final paycheck. Confirm with HR:
- Has my final tax settlement been processed?
- Will I receive a withholding tax receipt (원천징수영수증)?
- Is any tax refund owed to me?

**Freelancers / self-employed:** File a final income tax return at your district tax office (세무서) or via Hometax (hometax.go.kr) before departure. Deadline: within your tax filing period, or request early settlement.

**Tax refund:** If you overpaid taxes throughout the year, you'll receive a refund. This goes to your Korean bank account -- another reason to keep it open.

### 4. Housing Deposit Recovery (보증금 반환)

This is the #1 source of stress for departing foreigners.

**Timeline:**
- Notify your landlord in writing at least 30-60 days before move-out (check your contract for the exact notice period)
- The landlord is legally required to return your deposit on or before your move-out date
- In practice, landlords often delay until a new tenant is found

**Protect yourself:**
- Send written notice via **내용증명 (certified mail)** through the post office. This creates a legal record. Cost: ~3,000 KRW.
- Take timestamped photos/video of the apartment condition on move-out day
- Do a walkthrough with the landlord and agree on any deductions in writing
- Get a receipt for the returned deposit

**If the landlord won't return your deposit:**

1. **Send 내용증명** demanding return within 14 days
2. **File a complaint** at the local 주민센터 (community center) or 법률구조공단 (Korea Legal Aid Corporation, 132 hotline)
3. **Small claims court** (소액사건) for deposits under 30M KRW -- no lawyer needed, filing fee ~10,000 KRW
4. **Call 1345** (foreigner helpline) for interpretation and guidance

**If you're leaving Korea before the deposit is returned:**
- Appoint a trusted person (friend, colleague) with a **위임장 (power of attorney)** to handle it on your behalf
- Or arrange for bank transfer to your overseas account and get written confirmation from the landlord

> See also: `housing/jeonse-wolse.md` for deposit protection details

### 5. Bank Account

**Options:**
- **Keep it open** (recommended if awaiting pension refund, tax refund, or deposit return). Most Korean banks allow accounts to remain open after departure, but some features (online banking, transfers) may stop working without a Korean phone number.
- **Close it** if all refunds are settled. Visit your branch with ARC and passport. Transfer remaining balance overseas first via wire transfer or Wise.
- **Convert to overseas-accessible account:** Some banks (Woori, KEB Hana) offer overseas remittance-friendly setups. Ask at the branch before you leave.

**Important:** International wire transfers from Korean banks typically cost 10,000-25,000 KRW per transfer + intermediary fees. Use Wise or similar service for better rates.

### 6. Phone Contract

**Postpaid plan (후불제):**
- Visit your carrier's store (SK, KT, LG U+) with ARC and passport
- Pay any remaining balance and early termination fee (위약금) if within contract period
- Termination fees can be 100,000-300,000 KRW depending on remaining months

**Prepaid plan (선불제):**
- Usually no cancellation needed -- it expires automatically
- Contact your MVNO (Chingu, etc.) to confirm

**Keep your Korean number temporarily:** Some MVNOs allow you to keep a number active for a few months on a minimal plan. Useful if you still need Korean SMS verification for bank apps.

### 7. Alien Registration (외국인등록)

**At the airport on departure day:**
- You do NOT need to visit immigration beforehand if leaving permanently
- At airport immigration, inform the officer you are departing permanently
- Surrender your ARC (Residence Card) -- they will collect it
- Your departure is recorded, which triggers NPS refund processing

**If you plan to return to Korea later:** You can keep your ARC if your visa is still valid and you're just traveling temporarily. Only surrender if permanently leaving.

### 8. Severance Pay (퇴직금)

If you worked for the same employer for **1+ year continuously**, you are legally entitled to severance pay.

**Amount:** Roughly 1 month's average salary per year of employment.

**When paid:** Within 14 days of your last working day. If your employer doesn't pay, file a complaint at the **Ministry of Employment and Labor** (고용노동부, 1350 hotline).

### 9. Shipping & Moving

**Options for sending belongings home:**

| Method | Cost | Speed | Best For |
|--------|------|-------|----------|
| Korea Post (EMS) | ~30,000-80,000 KRW per box | 5-14 days | Small shipments, a few boxes |
| Sea freight (via CJ Logistics, Hanjin) | ~200,000-500,000 KRW | 4-8 weeks | Large shipments, furniture |
| Air freight | Expensive | 3-7 days | Urgent large items |
| Sell/donate locally | Free | Immediate | Furniture, appliances |

**Selling items:** Use 당근마켓 (Karrot) app -- very active, items sell fast, especially furniture and appliances. Post 2-3 weeks before departure.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Closing bank account too early** -- then pension refund has nowhere to go
2. **Not sending 내용증명 to landlord** -- verbal notice has no legal weight
3. **Forgetting pension refund** -- some people leave millions of KRW unclaimed
4. **Not checking pension reciprocity** -- applying for lump-sum when your country has a transfer agreement (you won't get cash)
5. **Ignoring phone contract termination fee** -- it doesn't go away, and can affect your credit if you return to Korea
