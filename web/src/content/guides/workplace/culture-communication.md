## Quick Answer

Korean workplaces run on **hierarchy, indirect communication (nunchi), and group harmony**. The biggest adjustment is learning to read what's NOT said. Direct confrontation is avoided. Seniority matters more than job title. Saying "no" directly to a superior is culturally jarring. Understanding the system helps you navigate it -- and Korean labor law protects you.

---

## Core Concepts

### Nunchi (눈치) -- Reading the Room

The ability to sense others' feelings without being told explicitly.

- "This might be a bit difficult" often means **no**
- "I'll think about it" usually means **they won't do it**
- "It's fine" with a tight smile means **it's not fine**
- "Let's do it next time" means **it's not happening**

### Hierarchy (위계질서)

Age and tenure determine rank, often more than job title.

- Use both hands when giving/receiving items to superiors
- Pour drinks for seniors at company dinners
- Don't leave before your boss unless you say "먼저 가보겠습니다"
- The most senior person speaks first and last in meetings

### Hoesik (회식) -- Company Dinners

Not optional socializing -- it's a workplace obligation. Dinner + drinks, often at Korean BBQ, then a "2차" (second round). You don't have to drink alcohol. To decline gracefully: "I'm not feeling well today" or attend dinner but skip the second round.

## Common Foreigner Pain Points

### Indirect Communication
Reframe questions to make "no" easier. Watch for soft refusals. Follow up important decisions in writing.

### Being Excluded from Information
Build a relationship with one Korean colleague as your "information bridge." Ask to join KakaoTalk group chats.

### Overwork & Boundaries
Maximum legal working hours: 52/week. Overtime must be compensated at 1.5x pay. Your contract defines your duties -- tasks outside it can be refused.

## For English Teachers: Hagwon Red Flags

| Red Flag | What It Means |
|----------|--------------|
| "We're like family here" | Boundary violations incoming |
| No written contract before arrival | Illegal |
| "Sometimes we need flexibility with hours" | Unpaid overtime |
| High teacher turnover | Bad management |
| Work on tourist visa | Illegal, you get deported |

## Key Workplace Korean Phrases

| Situation | Korean | Meaning |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Arriving at work | 출근했습니다 | I've arrived |
| Leaving before boss | 먼저 가보겠습니다 | I'll head out first |
| Acknowledging a task | 네, 알겠습니다 | Yes, understood |
| Declining politely | 죄송하지만 이번에는 어려울 것 같습니다 | Sorry, this time is difficult |

## Legal Protections

Korean labor law applies equally to foreign workers:
- **Workplace bullying** (illegal since 2019)
- **Discrimination** based on nationality is prohibited
- **Wage theft** can be reported to the labor ministry
- **Wrongful termination** requires 30 days notice or pay

**Where to get help:** Ministry of Employment and Labor (**1350**), Seoul Global Center, Legal Aid Corporation (**132**), Foreigner Helpline (**1345**).
