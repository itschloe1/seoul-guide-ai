## Quick Answer

Leaving Korea requires unwinding everything you set up: **pension refund, health insurance, tax settlement, bank account, phone contract, housing deposit, and alien registration**. Start at least **60 days before departure**. The biggest money items are your **National Pension lump-sum refund** and your **housing deposit**.

---

## The Timeline

| When | What |
|------|------|
| 60 days before | Notify landlord of move-out date |
| 45 days before | Start pension refund paperwork |
| 30 days before | Notify employer, check severance |
| 30 days before | Cancel or transfer phone contract |
| 14 days before | File final tax return if needed |
| 7 days before | Close or convert bank account |
| Departure day | Surrender ARC at airport immigration |

---

## National Pension Refund

If you paid into NPS, you're entitled to a **lump-sum refund** when leaving. Your total contributions + employer matching + interest. Can be significant -- 2 years at 2.5M KRW/month yields roughly 5-6M KRW back.

**Countries with transfer agreements** (US, Canada, Germany, France, Australia, Japan, UK): pension credits transfer instead of cash refund.

---

## Housing Deposit Recovery

Send written notice via certified mail (내용증명) at least 30-60 days before move-out. Take photos on move-out day. If landlord won't return deposit: small claims court for under 30M KRW.

---

## Severance Pay

If you worked 1+ year continuously, you're legally entitled to roughly 1 month's salary per year. Must be paid within 14 days of last working day.

---

## Common Mistakes

1. Closing bank account too early (pension refund has nowhere to go)
2. Not sending certified mail to landlord
3. Forgetting pension refund (millions of KRW unclaimed)
4. Ignoring phone contract termination fee
