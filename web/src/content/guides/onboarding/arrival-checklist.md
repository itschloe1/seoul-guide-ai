## Quick Answer

Moving to Korea as a foreigner follows a strict sequence: get a temporary SIM, book your ARC appointment, receive your card, open a bank account, then switch to a real phone plan. The whole administrative setup takes 4-6 weeks from landing. Mess up the order and you will waste weeks waiting.

**The critical dependency chain:**
Visa → Temporary SIM → ARC appointment → ARC card → Bank account → Postpaid phone → Full access

---

## Before You Arrive

### Prepare Documents (Carry-On, Not Checked Luggage)

- Passport (valid 6+ months)
- Visa Grant Notice
- Passport-size photos (3.5 x 4.5 cm, 4-5 copies)
- Employment contract or admission letter
- Proof of accommodation
- Cash: at least 500,000 KRW (~$370 USD)
- Unlocked phone for Korean SIM cards
- Travel insurance for first 1-3 months

### Download Essential Apps

| App | Purpose |
|-----|---------|
| Naver Map | Navigation (Google Maps doesn't work properly in Korea) |
| KakaoTalk | Messaging (Korea's WhatsApp) |
| Papago | Translation (better than Google Translate for Korean) |
| Kakao T | Taxi hailing |

## Day 1: Airport

1. **Buy a prepaid SIM** with a Korean phone number at the airport counter (30,000-55,000 KRW)
2. **Get a T-money card** at a convenience store (2,500 KRW)
3. **Get to your accommodation** -- AREX Express to Seoul Station (11,000 KRW, 43 min)

---

## Week 1: Book Your ARC Appointment

Do this on Day 1-2. Slots fill up fast. Book at [hikorea.go.kr](https://www.hikorea.go.kr).

**Documents to prepare:** Application form, passport + copy, color photo, proof of residence, fee (30,000-35,000 KRW cash).

---

## Week 2-4: ARC and Bank Account

1. **Attend ARC appointment** -- arrive 20 minutes early with all documents and cash
2. **Wait 2-4 weeks** for card processing
3. **Open bank account** once you have your ARC -- Hana Bank, KB Kookmin, or Shinhan recommended

### Bank Account Documents Needed
- ARC + Passport
- Korean phone number
- Proof of address
- Employment contract (some banks)
- Initial deposit: 1,000-10,000 KRW

---

## Week 3-5: Switch to Postpaid Phone

Visit a carrier store with ARC, passport, and Korean bank card. Request **number portability** to keep your prepaid number.

---

## Month 1-2: Settling In

- **Find permanent housing** (wolse is recommended for foreigners)
- **Register for NHIS** health insurance
- **Register your address** at the district office within 14 days of moving
- **Set up internet** at home (KT recommended for English support)
