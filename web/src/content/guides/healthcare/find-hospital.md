## Quick Answer

Korea has excellent healthcare at prices that shock most Westerners (in a good way). You can walk into most clinics without an appointment, you don't need a GP referral to see a specialist, and major hospitals in Seoul all have International Healthcare Centers with English-speaking staff. For emergencies, call **119** (ambulance is free). For non-urgent questions, call **1339** or **1330** (24/7, English available).

---

## How Korean Healthcare Works

- **No GP gatekeeping.** Walk directly into any specialist clinic
- **Walk-in culture.** Most neighborhood clinics accept walk-ins
- **Separated prescribing and dispensing.** Take your prescription to any pharmacy
- **Fast.** Appointments are 5-15 minutes
- **Affordable.** Dramatically cheaper than the US, UK, or most of Europe

## Emergency (119)

1. Call **119** -- ambulance, fire, and rescue
2. Say "English, please" for interpreter connection
3. Ambulance is **free** -- no charge for the ride
4. Major hospital ERs have English-speaking staff

## Pharmacy System

Doctors prescribe, pharmacies dispense. Take your prescription (처방전) to any pharmacy (약국, marked with a green cross). Prescription valid for 3 days (acute) or 30 days (chronic). **Foreign prescriptions are not accepted** -- you must see a Korean doctor.

## Common Questions

**Q: Do I need a referral to see a specialist?**
No. You can go directly to any specialist. Exception: to get lower NHI copay at a university hospital, a referral helps.

**Q: Is the ambulance really free?**
Yes. You only pay for treatment at the hospital.

**Q: Can I use my home country's prescription?**
No. Korean pharmacies only accept Korean prescriptions. Bring your medication list to show a Korean doctor.
