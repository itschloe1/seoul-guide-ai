## Quick Answer

Korea has universal healthcare through the **National Health Insurance Service (NHIS)**. If you stay longer than 6 months, you must enroll. NHIS covers roughly 60-80% of medical costs. Monthly premium is around 70,000-150,000 KRW depending on your status. The biggest trap: there's a gap period before coverage starts where you have zero coverage unless you arrange private or travel insurance.

---

## Who Must Enroll

All foreigners residing in Korea for 6 months or longer must enroll. This is not optional.

- **Employed:** Immediately upon ARC issuance + employment start
- **Students (D-2, D-4):** Mandatory upon alien registration
- **Self-employed:** After 6 months of continuous residence

NHIS payment status is checked during visa renewals. Unpaid premiums can block your visa extension.

---

## Monthly Premiums

### Employed (50/50 split with employer)
Health Insurance: 7.19% of monthly wage total. **Example:** 3,000,000 KRW salary = ~115,000 KRW your share.

### Self-Employed
Based on income/property/vehicle. Minimum ~114,000 KRW/month for new arrivals.

### Students
Reduced rate: ~76,390 KRW/month (2025).

## What's Covered

| Facility Type | Your Copay |
|--------------|------------|
| Local clinic | 30% |
| Hospital | 40% |
| General hospital | 50% outpatient |
| University hospital | 60% outpatient |
| Inpatient (any) | ~20% |
| Emergency | ~10% |
| Prescriptions | 20-30% |

**Covered:** Outpatient, inpatient, emergency, prescriptions, surgery, diagnostic imaging, mental health, basic dental, maternity, oriental medicine, free biennial health checkups.

**Not covered:** Cosmetic surgery, teeth whitening, most dental implants, orthodontics, glasses, non-emergency overseas treatment.

---

## The Gap Period (Before NHI)

If not employed, NHIS coverage doesn't start until 6 months of residence. During this gap, you have **zero health insurance**.

### Options During the Gap

1. **Travel insurance** (SafetyWing ~$45-70/month, World Nomads, Allianz)
2. **Private international insurance** (Cigna, Bupa, Aetna -- $100-300/month)
3. **Korean short-term insurance** (Samsung Fire, Meritz -- available at airport kiosks)
4. **Pay out of pocket** (risky but basic clinic visits are only 30,000-50,000 KRW)

---

## How to Enroll

### If Employed
Your employer handles everything within 14 days. Premium is auto-deducted from paycheck.

### If Self-Employed
Visit your local NHIS branch with ARC, passport, proof of address, and income documentation. Set up automatic bank transfer.

NHIS hotline: **1577-1000** (press 7 for English).

---

## Leaving Korea

- **Under 30 days:** No action needed, coverage continues
- **Over 30 days:** Suspend coverage (no premiums) or continue paying to avoid re-enrollment wait
- **Permanent departure:** Settle all premiums before leaving. Health insurance premiums are generally NOT refundable (unlike pension)

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I keep my home country's insurance instead?**
Possibly. Submit proof of equivalent coverage to your local NHIS branch for an exemption.

**Q: My employer isn't enrolling me. Is that legal?**
No. Report to NHIS (1577-1000) or Ministry of Employment and Labor (1350).

**Q: Is dental cleaning free?**
NHIS covers one scaling per year with a small copay (~10,000-15,000 KRW).
