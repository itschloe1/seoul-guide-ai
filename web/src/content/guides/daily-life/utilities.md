## Quick Answer

**Monthly utility costs for a single person in Korea:**

| Utility | Annual Average |
|---------|---------------|
| Electricity | ~30,000 KRW |
| City Gas | ~50,000 KRW |
| Water | ~10,000 KRW |
| Internet | ~40,000 KRW |
| Mobile | ~40,000 KRW |
| **Total** | **~130,000-200,000 KRW/mo** |

Electricity spikes in summer (air conditioning). Gas spikes in winter (floor heating). Budget 150,000-250,000 KRW/month for all utilities combined.

---

## Electricity (KEPCO)

KEPCO is the sole residential electricity provider. Korea uses a **progressive pricing system** -- the more you use, the higher the rate. Average residential rate: ~156 KRW/kWh.

**How to set up:** Call **123** (KEPCO hotline, 24/7). Provide ARC number, address, and move-in date.

---

## Gas (Floor Heating & Hot Water)

Gas is the biggest winter cost. Floor heating (ondol) is gas-powered, and winter bills can be 10-15x summer bills.

- **Summer:** Under 10,000 KRW/month
- **Winter (Dec-Feb):** 70,000-150,000 KRW/month

**Tip:** Set your boiler to moderate and use "away mode" when out. Insulate windows with draft stoppers.

## Internet

Korea has some of the fastest internet in the world. Three major ISPs offer fiber-optic:

| Provider | English Support | Monthly Cost (3-year) |
|----------|----------------|----------------------|
| KT (olleh) | Best for foreigners | 35,750-45,650 KRW |
| SK Broadband | Limited | Similar |
| LG U+ | Limited | Similar |

**KT Global Shop** (globalshop.kt.com) is the most reliable foreigner-friendly option with English consultation.

## Trash & Recycling

Korea has one of the world's strictest waste sorting systems. Violations carry real fines (50,000-1,000,000 KRW).

### Key Rules

- **Buy official trash bags** (종량제 봉투) for your specific district at convenience stores
- **Food waste** goes in separate yellow bags or RFID smart bins
- **Recyclables** go in clear bags -- must be clean, dry, and empty
- **Disposal times:** Between sunset and midnight (putting trash out in daytime is illegal)

### The "Pig Rule" for Food Waste
If a pig can eat it, it is food waste. Bones, shells, seeds, coffee grounds go in general waste.

## Common Questions

**Q: I just moved in. What do I set up first?**
Priority: (1) Electricity, call 123. (2) Gas, call regional provider. (3) Water, call 120. (4) Internet, order online.

**Q: Can I pay bills without a Korean bank account?**
Yes. Take paper bills to any convenience store and pay with cash or card.
