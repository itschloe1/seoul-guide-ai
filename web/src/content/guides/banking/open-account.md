## Quick Answer

You need a **Korean phone number** and an **Alien Registration Card (ARC)** to open a full bank account. Without an ARC, some banks will open a **restricted account** using just your passport, but expect low transfer limits and limited features.

**Fastest path:** Buy a prepaid SIM (no ARC needed) at the airport or online, then visit a foreigner-friendly bank branch with your passport and ARC. The whole process takes 30-60 minutes. Walk out with an account number, a debit (check) card, and mobile banking set up.

If you don't have an ARC yet, Woori Bank and Hana Bank have historically been the most willing to open limited passport-only accounts.

## The Catch-22 (And How to Solve It)

### The Problem

Korea has a circular dependency that trips up every new foreigner:

1. **To open a bank account**, you need a Korean phone number (for SMS verification and mobile banking).
2. **To get a postpaid phone plan**, you need a Korean bank account (for automatic monthly billing).
3. **To get an ARC**, you need to apply at Immigration after arriving, and it takes 2-3 weeks.

So you need a phone for the bank, a bank for the phone, and an ARC for both. Classic deadlock.

### The Solution: Break the Cycle in This Order

**Step 1: Get a prepaid SIM or eSIM (Day 1)**

Prepaid SIMs require only a passport. No ARC, no bank account needed.

| Option | Price | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Chingu Mobile (MVNO) | ~25,000 KRW/30 days | Unlimited data at 3 Mbps. Order online, pick up at airport or branch. |
| Airport kiosk SIM | ~50,000-65,000 KRW/30 days | Overpriced but convenient. |
| eSIM (Saily, Airalo, etc.) | ~$4-8/7-30 days | Instant activation. Some provide a Korean number, some don't. |

Important: Not all prepaid SIMs provide a Korean phone number that banks accept for SMS verification. Chingu Mobile does. Data-only eSIMs often do not.

**Step 2: Apply for your ARC at Immigration (Week 1)**

Apply as soon as possible. Processing takes roughly 2-3 weeks. Since March 2025, a **mobile ARC** (via the "Mobile Foreigner Residence Card" app) is accepted at major banks.

**Step 3: Open your bank account (Once you have ARC or mobile ARC)**

Bring your passport, ARC (physical or mobile), prepaid phone number, and proof of address to a foreigner-friendly branch.

**Step 4: Switch to a postpaid phone plan (Optional)**

Once you have a bank account, you can upgrade to a full postpaid plan.

### Hana Bank

The top recommendation for foreigners across most English-language guides. Legacy of KEB (Korea Exchange Bank), which specialized in foreign exchange. **Hana EZ** app is widely regarded as the best English-language banking app in Korea.

### KB Kookmin Bank

Korea's largest bank by customer base. Branches and ATMs everywhere. **KB Star Banking** app supports 11 languages. Dedicated foreign customer service center.

### Shinhan Bank

Strong brand in Seoul. Popular with students and younger expats. Was among the first to accept the mobile ARC (March 2025).

### Woori Bank

Historically one of the most willing banks to open accounts for foreigners without an ARC. 127-year history.

### NH NongHyup Bank

Unmatched branch and ATM reach outside Seoul. Choose NH if you live outside major cities.

## Without ARC: Restricted Account

If you don't have an ARC yet, some banks will open a **restricted (limited) account** using only your passport. This is not a full account.

**Which Banks Do It?**
- **Woori Bank:** Most consistently reported as accepting passport-only accounts
- **Hana Bank:** Has historically opened limited accounts with passport only

**Restrictions:** Low daily transfer limits, international remittance may be blocked, online/mobile banking may be unavailable.

**How to Upgrade:** Once your ARC arrives, visit the same branch with your ARC and passport. They will upgrade your account to full status in 15-30 minutes.

## Step-by-Step Process

1. **Choose your bank** based on the comparison above
2. **Pick the right branch** -- go to branches in business districts, university areas, or near immigration offices
3. **Go early** -- banks open at 9:00 AM and close at 4:00 PM, Monday-Friday
4. **Enter and take a number** -- say "I'd like to open a new bank account"
5. **Present your documents** -- the teller will ask your purpose (salary, living expenses, tuition)
6. **Set up mobile banking before leaving** -- this is critical
7. **Make your initial deposit** -- 10,000 KRW is standard
8. **Receive your bankbook and card**

**Total time:** 30-60 minutes.

## Common Problems & Solutions

### Transfer Limits on New Accounts
Visit the branch with your employment contract, rental contract, or university enrollment to raise your limits.

### Phone Number Issues
Use Chingu Mobile or similar MVNOs that register the SIM to your passport. For full "real-name verification," some banks require a postpaid number.

### Branch Refusal
Try a different branch of the same bank, especially a larger one in an expat-heavy area. Bring a Korean-speaking friend if language is the barrier.

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I open a bank account as a tourist?**
Technically possible at some banks as a restricted account with passport only, but increasingly difficult. Most guides recommend waiting until you have an ARC.

**Q: How long does it take?**
30-60 minutes at the branch. You walk out with an account number same-day.

**Q: Can I open an account online?**
No. First-time foreign account holders must visit a branch in person.

**Q: Do I need to speak Korean?**
Not at foreigner-friendly branches. Hana, Shinhan, and KB branches in major areas usually have English-speaking staff.

**Q: Is my money safe?**
Deposits are protected up to 50,000,000 KRW per depositor per bank by the Korea Deposit Insurance Corporation (KDIC). This applies to foreigners.
