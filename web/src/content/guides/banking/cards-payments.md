## Quick Answer

Korea is roughly 85% cashless. Visa and Mastercard work at most chain stores, hotels, and restaurants, but fail at traditional markets, street vendors, small local shops, and almost all online Korean sites. Your best bet as a foreigner:

- **Tourists (no ARC):** Get a WOWPASS or Namane prepaid card at the airport. Carry 50,000-200,000 KRW cash for markets and street food.
- **Residents (with ARC):** Open a Korean bank account, get a check (debit) card, set up Kakao Pay and Naver Pay. You now function like a local.
- **Online shopping:** Use Gmarket Global (accepts foreign cards) or get a Korean check card for Coupang.

## Why Foreign Cards Fail

Korea's domestic payment infrastructure is fundamentally different from international networks. Most Korean payment terminals communicate through a local VAN system, not through global Visa/Mastercard rails.

| Venue Type | Foreign Card Acceptance |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Hotels, airports | 95%+ |
| Convenience stores | 90%+ |
| Department stores | 95%+ |
| Chain restaurants | 90%+ |
| Small local restaurants | 70-80% |
| Traditional markets | 20-30% |
| Street food vendors | ~5% |
| Online Korean shopping | Very low |

## Tourist-Friendly Cards

### WOWPASS

The most popular prepaid card for foreign visitors. Combines currency exchange, cashless payments, and T-money transit in one card.

- **Issue fee:** 5,000 KRW (~$3.50)
- **Where to get:** WOWPASS kiosks at airports, major subway stations (160+ locations)
- **Currencies accepted:** 16 currencies
- **Transit:** Works as T-money card (subway, bus, taxi)
- **Where it works:** Anywhere Korean check cards are accepted

### Namane Card

- **Issue fee:** 7,000 KRW
- **Unique feature:** Supports KTX high-speed trains (WOWPASS does not)
- **Transit system:** Rail+ (not T-money)
- **Custom design:** Upload any photo for your card face

## Mobile Payments

### Samsung Pay
Most foreigner-friendly mobile payment -- **no ARC or Korean bank account needed**. Works with international Visa or Mastercard. Uses both NFC and MST, meaning it works even on older magnetic stripe terminals.

### Apple Pay
Very limited in Korea. Only ~10% of stores have NFC terminals. Only works with Korean-issued Hyundai Cards.

### Kakao Pay & Naver Pay
Require the "Golden Triangle": ARC + Korean phone number + Korean bank account. Full features for residents, unavailable for tourists.

## Credit Cards for Foreigners

Requirements for a Korean credit card:
- ARC with at least 1 year remaining
- Korean bank account held for at least 3 months
- Proof of stable income
- First-time approvals typically come with low limits: 500,000 to 1,000,000 KRW

**Alternative:** Secured credit card -- deposit a lump sum as collateral and build Korean credit history.

## Common Questions

**Q: I just arrived with no ARC. What's the fastest way to pay?**
Get a WOWPASS card at the airport (takes 5 minutes). It works as both a payment card and transit card.

**Q: How do I pay for subway and bus?**
Get a T-money card (any convenience store, 2,500 KRW), WOWPASS, or Namane card. Foreign contactless cards do not work on transit yet.

**Q: Do I need to carry cash?**
Yes, keep at least 50,000 KRW on hand for traditional markets, street food, and small restaurants.
