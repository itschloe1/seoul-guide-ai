## Quick Answer

Choose **wolse (monthly rent)**. As a foreigner, jeonse is almost never the right call. The deposit is too large (often $150,000-$500,000+), the fraud risk is real, and you have fewer safety nets than Korean nationals. Wolse requires a manageable deposit of 5-10 million won plus monthly rent.

---

## Rental Types Explained

### Jeonse (전세) -- Lump-Sum Deposit, No Monthly Rent
Deposit 50-80% of property value (100-500M+ KRW in Seoul). Zero monthly rent. Landlord returns the full deposit after 2 years. **Risk:** If the landlord can't return your deposit, you're in serious trouble. As of December 2025, Korea has recognized 35,909 jeonse fraud victims.

### Wolse (월세) -- Deposit + Monthly Rent
Deposit 5-30M KRW plus 500,000-1,500,000 KRW/month. Standard lease is 1 year. **This is what most foreigners use.**

### Banjeonse (반전세) -- The Hybrid
Larger deposit (50-100M KRW) with reduced monthly rent (200,000-560,000 KRW). Best if you have substantial savings and want lower monthly costs.

## Which Should Foreigners Choose?

| Factor | Wolse | Jeonse |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Upfront cost | 5-30M KRW | 100-500M+ KRW |
| Monthly cost | 500K-1.5M KRW | 0 |
| Financial risk | Low | Very high |
| Fraud exposure | Low | Very high |
| Ease for foreigners | Easiest | Hardest |

## Jeonse Scam Warning

### Red Flags -- Walk Away If You See These
- Jeonse deposit unusually low compared to market value
- Landlord is not the registered owner
- Existing mortgages exceed property value
- Landlord pressures you to skip the fixed-date stamp
- Cash-only transactions
- Recently purchased property by landlord

### How to Check Property Registry
The property registry (등기부등본) shows ownership and debts. Get it from IROS (iros.go.kr), a district court, or your real estate agent. **Always check before signing.**

## Legal Protections

The Housing Lease Protection Act applies equally to foreigners who:
1. File a move-in report (전입신고)
2. Get a fixed-date stamp (확정일자)
3. Actually live in the property

**Key rights:** 2-year minimum lease, one renewal right (4 years total), 5% max annual rent increase, deposit priority protection.

---

## Getting Your Deposit Back

If the landlord delays:
1. Send certified mail (내용증명) demanding return
2. You can legally refuse to vacate until deposit is returned
3. File for leasehold registration order at district court
4. Small claims court for deposits under 30M KRW

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I get a jeonse loan as a foreigner?**
Practically very difficult. Most foreigners on E or D visas will not qualify.

**Q: What if I need to break my lease early?**
For wolse, you'll typically forfeit part of your deposit. For jeonse, you usually wait until a new tenant is found.
