## Quick Answer

Finding an apartment in Korea as a foreigner is doable but requires preparation:

1. **Decide your budget and area.** Seoul studios range from 300,000 to 1,200,000 KRW/month
2. **Browse listings.** Korean apps (Zigbang, Dabang) or English platforms (Ziptoss, FOHO, Enkostay)
3. **Visit with an agent.** They show properties for free, charge commission at contract signing
4. **Verify ownership.** Pull the building registry before paying anything
5. **Sign, stamp, register.** Get a fixed-date stamp and register your lease

Budget at least 2-4 weeks for the full process.

## Contract Guide

### Key Steps
1. Pull building registry (등기부등본) to verify ownership
2. Review and sign the contract (bilingual versions available through Ziptoss/FOHO)
3. Pay contract deposit (5-10% of total deposit) via bank transfer
4. Get fixed-date stamp (확정일자) at community center
5. Register your move-in (전입신고) within 14 days

### Red Flags
- Price too good to be true
- Agent refuses to show building registry
- Landlord wants cash payment
- Pressure to sign immediately

---

## Getting Your Deposit Back

- Take timestamped photos at move-in
- Give proper notice (1-2 months before lease end)
- Clean and restore the unit
- If landlord won't return deposit: send formal demand (내용증명), then file at district court

## Common Questions

**Q: Do I need an ARC to rent?**
For a standard lease, yes. For short-term stays (Enkostay, goshiwon), a passport is often sufficient.

**Q: Can I negotiate rent?**
Yes. Deposit, rent, and lease terms are all negotiable.

**Q: How long does the process take?**
2-4 weeks from first search to move-in.
