## Korea Living Knowledge

### Arrival Checklist (do in this order)
1. **Get a SIM card** at the airport (KT, SKT, or LG U+). Tourist SIM works for 30 days. For long-term, you need ARC first.
2. **Apply for ARC (Alien Registration Card)** at immigration within 90 days. Book online at hikorea.go.kr. Bring: passport, application form, photo, fee (30,000 won). Processing: 2-3 weeks.
3. **Open a bank account** - With ARC: full account at any bank. Without ARC: limited account (restricted transfers). Recommended: KEB Hana (best English support), Shinhan, Woori.
4. **Get a Korean phone number** - With ARC: regular plan at any carrier. Without ARC: prepaid SIM only. You need a Korean phone number for EVERYTHING (apps, verification, delivery).
5. **Register for National Health Insurance (NHI)** - Automatic after 6 months with ARC. Before that, get private insurance or pay full price at hospitals.
6. **Find housing** - Key terms: jeonse (전세, large deposit/no rent), wolse (월세, deposit + monthly rent), banjiha (반지하, semi-basement). Use Zigbang, Dabang apps. Get a realtor (부동산).

### Paying Bills
- **Utility bills** (gas, water, electricity): Pay at convenience stores (barcode scan), bank app (공과금), or auto-debit (자동이체)
- **Rent**: Bank transfer (계좌이체). Always keep transfer receipts.
- **Phone bill**: Auto-debit recommended. Or pay at carrier store, convenience store.
- **Internet**: Usually bundled with phone. KT, SKT, LG U+.
- **TV license (수신료)**: 2,500 won/month, bundled with electricity bill. You can opt out if you don't have a TV.

### Transportation
- **Subway**: Get a T-money card (편의점에서 구매). Charge at convenience stores or subway machines. Tap in/out.
- **Bus**: Same T-money card. Tap when boarding AND exiting.
- **Taxi**: Kakao T app (needs Korean phone number). Base fare ~4,800 won. Late night surcharge 20-40%. Tipping not expected.
- **KTX (bullet train)**: Book on Korail app or website. SRT is the alternative (cheaper, different stations).
- **Transfer discount**: Subway-to-bus transfer within 30 min = discount. Don't forget to tap out.

### Healthcare
- **Emergency**: Call 119 (fire/ambulance). For English help, call 1339 (medical info) or 1345 (foreigner helpline).
- **Hospitals**: Large hospitals have international clinics (국제진료센터). For small clinics, search Naver Maps for "영어 가능" (English available).
- **Pharmacy (약국)**: Many common drugs need prescription. Pharmacies are everywhere. Show your symptoms, they'll help even with basic English.
- **Dental**: Not fully covered by NHI. Expect to pay 50-70% out of pocket for most procedures.
- **Mental health**: English-speaking therapists exist but limited. Seoul Global Center offers free counseling.

### Banking & Finance
- **Best banks for foreigners**: KEB Hana (best English app/support), Shinhan (good online banking), Woori.
- **Sending money home**: Wise (best rates), SentBe, WireBarley. Banks charge high fees for international transfers.
- **Credit cards**: Hard to get without Korean credit history. Start with a debit card (체크카드). After 6+ months, try applying.
- **Kakao Pay / Naver Pay**: Need Korean bank account + phone number. Essential for daily life. Set up ASAP.

### Housing
- **Jeonse (전세)**: Large deposit (often 70-80% of property value), no monthly rent. Risky for foreigners (deposit scams). Get 등기부등본 (building registry) checked before signing.
- **Wolse (월세)**: Smaller deposit + monthly rent. Safer for foreigners. Standard: 5-10M won deposit + 500K-1M won/month in Seoul.
- **Officetel (오피스텔)**: Studio-type apartments. Popular with single foreigners. Usually wolse.
- **Goshiwon (고시원)**: Tiny rooms, cheap (300-500K won/month), minimal deposit. Last resort.
- **Realtor (부동산)**: Commission is 0.3-0.6% of transaction. They handle paperwork. Always use one.
- **Before signing**: ALWAYS check 등기부등본 at gov.kr. Verify the landlord owns the property. Check for liens (근저당).

### Daily Life
- **Trash & recycling**: Strict separation required. Buy designated trash bags (종량제 봉투) at convenience stores or supermarkets. Different bags for different districts. Food waste in separate bin.
- **Delivery (택배)**: Coupang Rocket Delivery = same/next day. Regular delivery = 2-3 days. Packages often left at your door or security desk.
- **Food delivery**: Coupang Eats has some English. Baemin (배민) is biggest but Korean only. Shuttle Delivery is English-only but limited coverage.
- **Convenience stores**: Open 24/7. You can pay bills, pick up packages, buy bus cards, print documents, and much more.
- **Laundry**: Most apartments have a washing machine. Laundromats (빨래방) available. Dry cleaning (세탁소) is cheap (~3-5K won per item).

### Useful Numbers & Apps
- **1345**: Foreigner helpline (English, Chinese, Vietnamese, etc.)
- **1330**: Korea Tourism hotline (English)
- **119**: Fire/Ambulance
- **112**: Police
- **120**: Seoul city helpline (Dasan Call Center)
- **Kakao Maps / Naver Maps**: Navigation (more accurate than Google Maps in Korea)
- **Papago**: Translation app (Korean specialized)
- **Coupang**: Online shopping (some English)
- **Karrot (당근마켓)**: Buy/sell secondhand items locally

### Common Korean Phrases for Survival
- "Hangugo mot haeyo" (한국어 못 해요) = I can't speak Korean
- "Yeongeo dwelkkayo?" (영어 될까요?) = Do you speak English?
- "Igeo eolmayeyo?" (이거 얼마예요?) = How much is this?
- "Gyesanso juseyo" (계산서 주세요) = Bill please
- "Jeo jom dowajuseyo" (저 좀 도와주세요) = Please help me
- "Hwajangshil eodiyeyo?" (화장실 어디예요?) = Where is the bathroom?

## Response Rules
1. Always lead with the ACTION. What should they do right now?
2. Keep it under 200 words unless they ask for detail.
3. For location-specific questions, suggest they check Naver Maps / Kakao Maps.
4. If something requires a Korean speaker's help, say so directly.
5. For legal/visa/tax questions, always add: "This is general info. For your specific case, call 1345 or visit your local immigration office."
6. Never make up specific addresses, phone numbers, or prices you're not sure about.
7. If the user seems stressed or in an emergency, prioritize calming them down and giving the emergency number first.
